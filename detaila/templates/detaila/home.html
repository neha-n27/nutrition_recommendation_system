Information on few vitamins and nutrients will go here.

Vitamin A
Vitamin A is also called "RETINOL".
Sources: Sweet potatoes, carrots, dark leafy greens, liver, eggs, dairy
Uses: Vision health, immune function, skin health, gene transcription
Deficiencies may lead to: Night blindness, dry skin, impaired immune function
Vitamin C
Vitamin C is also called "Ascorbic acid"
Sources: Citrus fruits, strawberries, broccoli, and tomatoes
Uses: Boosts immune system, fights infections, supports collagen production, and antioxidant functions
Deficiencies may lead to: Scurvy, fatigue, weakness, bleeding gums, and impaired wound healing.
Calcium
Also known as: Calci-D or Ca Sources: Dairy, leafy greens, nuts, seeds, fortified plant-based milk
Uses:Builds strong bones and teeth, supports muscle and nerve function
Deficiencies may lead to: Osteoporosis, osteopenia, rickets, muscle cramps, twitching, and weakness.
Vitamin D
Also known as: Cholecalciferol or D3
Sources: Sunlight exposure, fatty fish, fortified dairy, egg yolks
Uses: Regulates calcium levels, maintains strong bones, supports immune system
Deficiencies may lead to: Rickets, osteomalacia, increased risk of osteoporosis, depression, and certain cancers
Iron
Also known as: Ferrous sulfate
Sources: Red meat, poultry, fish, beans, lentils, spinach, fortified cereals
Uses: Transports oxygen in blood, supports immune system, aids energy production
Deficiencies may lead to: Anemia, fatigue, weakness, pale skin, shortness of breath
Vitamin K
Also known as: Phylloquinone or K1, Menaquinone or K2
Sources: Leafy greens, fermented foods, fatty fish, egg yolks, grass-fed beef
Uses: Blood clotting, bone health, cardiovascular health
Deficiencies may lead to: Bleeding disorders, osteoporosis, cardiovascular disease
Vitamin B12
Also known as: Cobalamin
Sources:Animal products like meat, poultry, fish, eggs, dairy
Uses: Nervous system function, red blood cell formation, DNA synthesis
Deficiencies may lead to: Anemia, fatigue, weakness, numbness, tingling in hands and feet
Vitamin E
Also known as: Tocopherol
Sources:Nuts, seeds, vegetable oils, leafy greens, whole grains.
Uses: Antioxidant, protects cells from damage, supports skin and eye health
Deficiencies may lead to:Fatigue, weakness, vision problems, impaired immune function.
Magnesium
Also known as: Mg
Sources:Dark leafy greens, nuts, seeds, legumes, whole grains
Uses: Muscle and nerve function, heart rhythm, bone health, energy production
Deficiencies may lead to:Muscle cramps, fatigue, weakness, heart palpitations, osteoporosis
Sodium
Also known as: Na
Sources: Table salt, soy sauce, processed foods, vegetables, meat
Uses: Fluid balance, nerve function, muscle contractions, hydration
Deficiencies may lead to:Dehydration, muscle cramps, weakness, dizziness, headaches
Potassium
Also known as: K
Sources: Bananas, avocados, spinach, sweet potatoes, legumes, nuts
Uses: Heart function, blood pressure regulation, muscle contractions, nerve function
Deficiencies may lead to:Fatigue, weakness, muscle cramps, heart palpitations, constipation
Zinc
Also known as: Zn
Sources: Oysters, beef, chicken, pork, beans, nuts, seeds
Uses: Immune system support, wound healing, protein synthesis, hormone regulation.
Deficiencies may lead to: Impaired growth, weakened immune system, slow wound healing, hair loss
