Tadasana (Mountain Pose)
Tadasana strengthens thighs, knees, and ankles. It firms the abdomen and buttocks, improves posture, and reduces flat feet.
Bhujangasana (Cobra Pose)
Bhujangasana helps to open up the shoulders and neck, strengthens the entire back and shoulders, improves flexibility of the upper and middle back, and invigorates the heart.
Adho Mukha Svanasana (Downward-Facing Dog Pose)
Adho Mukha Svanasana stretches the shoulders, hamstrings, calves, arches, and hands. It strengthens the arms and legs, improves digestion, relieves headache, insomnia, back pain, and fatigue.
Virabhadrasana I (Warrior Pose I)
Virabhadrasana I strengthens the legs, opens the hips and chest, and stretches the arms and legs. It improves focus, balance, and stability.
Virabhadrasana II (Warrior Pose II)
Virabhadrasana II strengthens the legs, stretches the groins, chest, and lungs, and increases stamina. It relieves backaches, stimulates abdominal organs, and improves circulation and respiration.
Balasana (Child's Pose)
Balasana gently stretches the hips, thighs, and ankles. It helps to calm the mind, relieve stress and fatigue, and can alleviate back and neck pain when done with extended arms.
Vrikshasana (Tree Pose)
Vrikshasana strengthens the legs, opens the hips, and improves balance and stability. It calms the mind, improves concentration, and strengthens ligaments and tendons of the feet.
Setu Bandhasana (Bridge Pose)
Setu Bandhasana stretches the chest, neck, and spine. It calms the brain and helps alleviate stress and mild depression. It stimulates abdominal organs, lungs, and thyroid glands.
Paschimottanasana (Seated Forward Bend)
Paschimottanasana stretches the spine, shoulders, and hamstrings. It stimulates the liver, kidneys, ovaries, and uterus. It improves digestion and relieves symptoms of menopause and menstrual discomfort.
Savasana (Corpse Pose)
Savasana relaxes the whole body, calms the mind, and reduces stress and fatigue. It improves concentration and sleep quality.
Utkatasana (Chair Pose)
Utkatasana strengthens the thighs, ankles, calves, and spine. It stretches the shoulders and chest. It stimulates the heart, diaphragm, and abdominal organs. It reduces flat feet and stimulates the circulatory system.
Ardha Matsyendrasana (Half Lord of the Fishes Pose)
Ardha Matsyendrasana increases the flexibility of the spine, hips, and neck. It massages the abdominal organs, improves digestion, and relieves menstrual discomfort.
Baddha Konasana (Bound Angle Pose)
Baddha Konasana stimulates abdominal organs, ovaries, prostate gland, bladder, and kidneys. It stretches the inner thighs, groins, and knees. It improves flexibility in the groin and hip region and reduces discomfort during menstruation and menopause.
Matsyasana (Fish Pose)
Matsyasana stretches the deep hip flexors and intercostal muscles between the ribs. It stretches and stimulates the muscles of the front of the neck and throat, the deep hip flexors, and the muscles between the ribs. It stimulates the organs of the belly and throat.
Garudasana (Eagle Pose)
Garudasana strengthens and stretches the ankles and calves. It stretches the thighs, hips, shoulders, and upper back. It improves concentration, balance, and flexibility. It also stretches the muscles, tendons, and ligaments of the legs and shoulders, and the hips, thighs, shoulders, and upper back.
