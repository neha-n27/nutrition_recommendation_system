Surya Namaskar Steps

Step 1
Pranamasana (Prayer Pose)
Stand at the edge of your mat, keep your feet together and balance your weight equally on both feet. Expand your chest and relax your shoulders. As you breathe in, lift both arms up from the sides, and as you exhale, bring your palms together in front of the chest in a prayer position.
Step 2
Hasta Uttanasana (Raised Arms Pose)
As you inhale, lift the arms up and back, keeping the biceps close to the ears. In this pose, the objective is to stretch the whole body up from the heels to the tips of the fingers.
Step 3
Hasta Padasana (Hand to Foot Pose)
Exhale and gradually bend forward, folding from your waist. Focus on bending the upper portion of your body and keep your spine straight as you lower yourself toward the ground. Hasta Padasana helps balance the mind and improves digestion.
Step 4
Ashwa Sanchalanasana (Equestrian Pose)
Inhale deeply as you stretch your body parallel to the floor. Extend your arms along your sides and bring your right knee towards the right side of your chest. Arch your back gently and look upward, simultaneously extending your left leg behind you, developing confidence and willpower with the pose.
Step 5
Chaturanga Dandasana (Four-Limbed Staff Pose)
Sit with your legs together and stretched out in front of you. Place your hands next to your hips on the floor. Flex your feet and extend out through the heels. Engage your thighs and actively press your sitting bones down.
Step 6
Ashtanga Namaskara (Eight-Limbed Pose)
Lower your knees to the floor. Exhale and bring your chest and chin to the floor, keeping your hips slightly off the ground. Ensure your elbows are tucked in, and your toes, knees, chest, and chin touch the floor together.
Step 7
Bhujangasana (Cobra Pose)
Lower your hips and legs to the floor. Inhale and lift your chest off the floor, keeping your elbows bent and close to your body. Look up gently and ensure your navel remains on the floor.
Step 8
Adho Mukha Svanasana (Downward-Facing Dog Pose)
Lift your hips and straighten your knees, forming an inverted V-shape with your body. Ensure your palms are pressed firmly into the ground, your head is between your arms, and your heels are touching the floor.
Step 9
Ashwa Sanchalanasana (Equestrian Pose)
Bring your right foot forward between your hands and lower your left knee to the floor. Look up, arch your back slightly, and bring your right knee towards your chest, similar to Step 4 but with the opposite leg.
Step 10
Hasta Padasana (Hand to Foot Pose)
Exhale and gradually bend forward, folding from your waist. Focus on bending the upper portion of your body and keep your spine straight as you lower yourself toward the ground, similar to Step 3.
Step 11
Hasta Uttanasana (Raised Arms Pose)
As you inhale, lift the arms up and back, keeping the biceps close to the ears. In this pose, the objective is to stretch the whole body up from the heels to the tips of the fingers, similar to Step 2.
Step 12
Pranamasana (Prayer Pose)
Exhale and stand straight. Bring your arms down and keep your palms together in front of your chest, similar to Step 1. This completes one set of Surya Namaskar.
